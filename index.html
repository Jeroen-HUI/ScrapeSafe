For people who drive low

Ground clearance on the map, with people who get it.

ScrapeSafe is for drivers who think about scrapes and want smarter road awareness. Here, “scrape” means clearance and respect for the road, not driving like a fool. It is still on you to pay attention, follow local rules, and use common sense.

  • A map with crowdsourced hazards (bumps, tables, inclines, and more). Coverage depends on area; it will never be complete.
  • Routing and navigation aimed at informed drives, not only the fastest A to B line.
  • A real community layer: garage, feed, groups, meets, and messaging around actual drives.

How it works

Three steps from opening the app to driving with clearer context. You are still responsible on the road.

1. Discover

See the map, not just a line

Explore hazards and community markers, open details, and get a feel for what others reported. It will never be complete, so stay sharp.

2. Plan

Route with awareness

Build a plan that fits how you drive: clearance-minded routing and cues alongside navigation. We are not promising zero contact on every street.

3. Drive

Stay in touch with your crew

Meets, groups, and messages keep the drive tied to people you trust. Slow down for conditions and other road users.

What you get in the app

Dark UI with a teal “signal” accent on iOS and Android phones. Tabs and tools match what you see on your device.

Map

Live map and hazard context

Work with speed bumps, speed tables, inclines, and other signals. Add what you spot, with verification and moderation in the app. No product can cover every road perfectly.

Routing

Navigation that respects clearance

Turn-by-turn with hazard callouts where data exists. It is a different job than what Google Maps or Waze optimize for; when our backends and your settings allow, we lean toward smoother, better-informed lines.

Clear

“Can I clear it?” helper

Model a bump or incline with heights and angles, plus optional tilt sampling, to estimate whether your bumper likely clears. It is a tool, not a lab-grade guarantee.

Garage

Your cars, photos, mods

Save clearance-related fields and build notes so tools and navigation line up with how the car actually sits.

Feed

Feed and stories

Share builds and moments, discover others, and keep the app from feeling like noise. What people write stays as they wrote it (no auto-translation of user posts in the app).

Groups

Groups and smaller circles

Create or join communities around cities, styles, or crews, with private meets and posts where you set them.

Meets

Meets and events

Host or join rolling meets on the map, including group-only options so the right people see the invite.

Messages

DMs and group chats

Coordinate starts, routes, and last-minute changes in threads tied to real profiles, not random spam blasts.

Safety, privacy, realism

We take driving seriously. The app adds context; it does not replace what you must still watch for yourself.

  • No full hazard coverage. Markers depend on users, time, and moderation. Roads change, and gaps will exist.
  • No “guaranteed safe route.” Weather, traffic, roadworks, and mistakes still happen. Use your eyes.
  • Privacy in the product: accounts, clear policies, and flows like delete-account. Read our Privacy Policy for what we collect and why.
  • Location, notifications, and media prompts follow platform rules. Foreground and background use is explained for navigation and alerts.
Pro is optional. The free tier is supported by the same product mix you see in your region.

FAQ

Honest answers if you are comparing us to other maps, or searching for things like “low car routing” and “speed bump map”.

What is ScrapeSafe in one line?

A dark-themed phone app that combines map hazard context, clearance-minded navigation, and a driving community (garage, feed, groups, meets, DMs) for people who care about ground clearance.

Is ScrapeSafe another Google Maps or Waze?

Those apps are great for general traffic and ETA. ScrapeSafe adds clearance-oriented context and social features for a specific crowd. You can still use other maps wherever they fit. We are not part of Google or Waze.

Does the map show every speed bump?

No. Data is crowdsourced and moderated. Some regions are packed; others are thin. Slow down and look ahead, especially at night or in rain.

Does navigation guarantee I will not scrape?

No. Routing and callouts help with surprises; they do not replace your car’s limits, load, tyre pressure, or your own judgment.

What does the “Clear” tool do?

It estimates likely clearance over a modeled bump or incline from numbers you enter (and optional phone tilt). Think of it as guidance, not a certified measurement.

Who can see my garage and posts?

Visibility follows your account and where you post (feed, groups, meets). Check in-app controls and our Privacy Policy.

Is there a subscription?

The app may offer ScrapeSafe Pro (and related entitlements) with billing through the App Store or Google Play. Exact prices and benefits are on the store page where you download.

What is ScrapeSafe Business?

Business is the shop and organizer tier: everything Pro-equivalent, plus business-facing tools such as a map pin, highlights, insights, WhatsApp or contact shortcuts, and strong meet-hosting for organizers (see in-app copy for limits). Some people get a business badge at signup (business account in our backend); the paid Business subscription turns on the full business toolkit. Check your profile or entitlements in the app.

Languages and units?

Many builds ship English and Dutch in the UI, plus speed units like km/h and mph. Community text stays in whatever language the author used.

How do I report a safety or abuse issue?

Use in-app reporting where it exists and email hello@scrapesafe.app for account or policy questions.
